This one’s golden...Vibe coding rules

**Save it. Use it. Improve it.**

I just came across a solid piece of garage tinkering that deserves to live in everyone’s toolkit: vibe-code-rules.md. [@Noah Taylor](https://www.skool.com/@noah-taylor-7495?g=quantum-quill-lyceum-1116&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/@noah-taylor-7495?g=quantum-quill-lyceum-1116&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03"), the author built a single source of truth for best practices across their vibe coding projects — lightweight, repeatable rules that keep the slop out and the consistency in. It’s not trying to be production gospel or some bloated framework. It’s exactly the kind of practical, no-fluff guardrail that most of us grinding agentic workflows actually need.What I love about it:

- It treats the filesystem as the architecture (folders and markdown doing the heavy lifting)
- Clear separation between “vibe coding” projects and everything else
- Honest amateur perspective with a built-in call for feedback from more experienced folks
- Smart rename and positioning relative to Jake’s ICM layers (global rules handed to the orchestrating agent)

This is the kind of thing that stops the constant reinvention and lets you focus on directing the work instead of managing chaos.

If you’re building with agents — especially if you’re still early in the ICM classroom — drop this into your repo and give it a spin. Then come back and tell us what broke, what clicked, or what you’d tweak.

Big thanks to Noah for shipping this openly. That’s how the collective stack evolves.

Star it if it helps you. Fork it if you make it better. Let’s keep building brains that can’t be taken away from us.
