LESSON 1.1: Welcome

## 📝 What This Course Is

I have been teaching AI in education and business for quite a few years. Before most people knew what a context window was. Before "prompt engineering" became a job title. Before anyone was talking about agent architecture or structured workflows.

I recorded videos. I wrote articles. I made predictions. I built frameworks.

This course puts all of that on the table and asks a simple question: did it hold up?

The answer, mostly, is yes. The core ideas landed. The predictions tracked. The frameworks evolved into the methodology I teach today across thousands of professionals, a 12,500-member community, and a peer-reviewed papers.

But "mostly" matters. Some things changed. Some tools I recommended are renamed. Some advice I gave was right in direction but early in detail. This course is honest about all of it. The revisions are a feature. They show the thinking is alive and still moving.

## 📐 How the Course Is Built

> Three modules. Each one does something different.

## Module 1: The Setup (you are here)

This lesson frames the course. The next lesson, "A 2,000-Year Overnight Success," gives you the full history of thinking machines from ancient Alexandria to the language models on your phone. It matters because the videos in Module 2 are part of a pattern that is two thousand years old. Every generation builds thinking machines, normalizes them, and forgets they were ever impossible. Understanding that pattern changes how you hear everything that comes after.

## Module 2: Old But Gold

Three videos from late 2023 and early 2024. Each one is paired with a written companion I wrote in 2026 that walks through what I got right, what needs updating, and how the ideas connect to what I teach now.

[LESSON 2.1: The Mindset Before the Method](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=10135ac3e904424d847b938004948ddb&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=10135ac3e904424d847b938004948ddb&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") A 39-minute workshop I gave to college professors at Flagler College in **January 2024**. The case that AI is a mindset problem, not a tool problem. The human becomes more valuable. The companion tracks how that thesis played out across two years of real training engagements.

[**Lesson 2.2: Raise the Bar**](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=79c815829450460eb106cd271a591d9f&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=79c815829450460eb106cd271a591d9f&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") **December 2023.** If students can use AI to cheat on your assignments, your assignments are too easy. The case for raising the baseline using the calculator, internet, and coding pyramid as historical proof. The companion adds the frameworks that turn "raise the bar" into a method.

[**Lesson 2.3: More Human**](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=723f6fbc980b4701b74bcb76615870c5&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=723f6fbc980b4701b74bcb76615870c5&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") **December 2023**. I told a story about saving a man's life on Mount Fuji during a typhoon and used it to argue that AI strips away the robotic parts of our work, leaving the human parts standing. The most personal video in this course. The companion connects it to the Ethics Engine research and the broader question of what AI reveals about us.

## Module 3: Where It Goes Now

Current articles on the questions that matter today. These are not retrospectives. This is where the early thinking leads.

[**Lesson 3.1: Beyond the Turing Test**](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116?utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116?utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") We already blew past the Turing Test. The next question is whether you can tell your AI from someone else's. AI personality, the Ethics Engine, consciousness as resonance, and the governance problem ahead.

[**Lesson 3.2: The Questions Schools Should Be Asking**](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=5d14e28de95d43b2a3960e0ebbbfd617&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=5d14e28de95d43b2a3960e0ebbbfd617&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") Intellectual property in education is broken. Students should graduate owning AI trained on their thinking. Teachers should own their methods. The structural argument for why ownership is the real AI-in-education question.

[**Lesson 3.3: The Real Cost of Knowledge**](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=7658a441afa9449e92951519e2c95e5a&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=7658a441afa9449e92951519e2c95e5a&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") The apocalypse laptop thought experiment. If the internet is gone and you have one solar-powered laptop, do you pick Wikipedia or a local AI model? The answer reframes everything about AI energy costs, local vs cloud, and how we deploy intelligence.

[**Lesson 3.4: The Rise of Computational Orchestration**](https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=ed8f01da061a4b76bfde21fc4dbd4d41&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03 "https://www.skool.com/quantum-quill-lyceum-1116/classroom/d7ae60cf?md=ed8f01da061a4b76bfde21fc4dbd4d41&utm_campaign=skool_link_classroom&utm_content=9a42d1d697754c499c3effeee8135e03") The discipline that emerged from everything else in this course. Knowing which type of computation to apply to which problem. The 60/30/10 pattern. Why most AI transformations fail. This is where the early videos were pointing all along.
