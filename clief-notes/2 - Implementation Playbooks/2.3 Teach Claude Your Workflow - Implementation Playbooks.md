2.3 Teach Claude Your Workflow

## What You'll Get From This

A repeatable shortcut that runs your browser tasks for you, triggered with a single command.

## The Lesson

## Step 1: Open the Claude Sidebar

Click the Claude icon in your Chrome toolbar. The sidebar opens.

---

## Step 2: Start Recording

Click the cursor icon in the menu bar. You can also type `/` in the chat bar and select "Record workflow."

Claude asks you to enable your microphone. Click "Enable microphone."

---

## Step 3: Do the Task and Talk Through It

This is the key part. You do the task while narrating what you are doing out loud. Claude watches your screen and listens to your voice at the same time.

Claude records:

- Pages you navigate to
- Buttons you click
- Links you follow
- Text you type into forms
- Checkboxes you select
- Dropdowns you choose from
- Scrolling you do
- What you say about each step

You do not need a script. Just talk naturally. Say what you are doing and why. For example:

- "I'm going to the reports tab."
- "Now I'm selecting this week's date range."
- "I'm clicking download to get the CSV."

The narration matters because it tells Claude your intent, not just the coordinates of your clicks. If a button moves or a page changes layout later, Claude can still figure out what you meant because you told it in plain language.

Work at a normal pace. You do not need to slow down much, but do not rush through the task faster than you can talk.

---

## Step 4: Stop Recording

When you finish the task, stop the recording in the Claude sidebar.

Claude generates three things from what it watched and heard:

- A name for the shortcut
- A prompt that describes what you did
- The starting URL

You can edit any of these before saving.

---

## Step 5: Run the Shortcut

Next time you need to do this task:

1. Open the Claude sidebar
2. Type `/` (forward slash)
3. Select your shortcut from the list
4. Claude runs through the workflow

You can watch Claude work or switch to another tab. Claude keeps going as long as Chrome is open.

---

## A Concrete Example: Weekly Report Download

Say you pull a report from a dashboard every Monday morning. Here is how to teach Claude to do it.

**Teach it once:**

1. Open Chrome and the Claude sidebar
2. Click the cursor icon to start recording
3. Enable your microphone
4. Navigate to your dashboard and say: "I'm going to the sales dashboard to pull the weekly report"
5. Log in if needed (Claude records the steps but not your password)
6. Click the Reports section and say: "This is where the reports live"
7. Select the date range and say: "I always pick the last seven days"
8. Click Download and say: "I download the CSV version"
9. Wait for the file to download
10. Stop the recording

Claude generates a shortcut. Review the name and prompt it created. Edit if needed. Save it.

**Run it every Monday:**

1. Open the Claude sidebar
2. Type `/weekly-sales-report` (or whatever you named it)
3. Claude opens the dashboard, navigates to reports, selects the dates, downloads the file
4. You drink your coffee

---

## Another Example: Checking a Website for Updates

Say you check a government site every few days to see if a form has been updated.

**Teach it:**

1. Start recording with your microphone on
2. Go to the site and say: "This is the licensing page I need to check"
3. Navigate to the page with the form
4. Scroll to where the version number or date appears and say: "I'm looking at this date here to see if anything changed"
5. Stop recording

**Run it:**

Type `/check-form-update` (or whatever you named it). Claude navigates to the page. You look at the version number.

You can also ask Claude to tell you what it sees:

```
Run "Check form update" and tell me the date shown on the page.
```

## Three Ways to Create Shortcuts

Recording a workflow is one way. There are two others.

**Convert a conversation to a shortcut.** If you have already walked Claude through a task across several chat messages, click the "Convert to task" button in the conversation header. Claude saves the whole exchange as a repeatable shortcut.

**Save a prompt as a shortcut.** If you sent Claude a well-written prompt that worked, hover over that message. An icon appears that lets you save the prompt directly as a shortcut.

All three methods produce the same thing: a reusable shortcut you trigger with `/`.

---

## Scheduling Shortcuts to Run Automatically

Once a shortcut works reliably, you can schedule it:

1. When creating or editing a shortcut, toggle on "Schedule" at the bottom
2. Choose the frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
3. Set the date and time
4. Choose which model to use
5. Save

Claude runs the task at the scheduled time. You get a notification when it finishes or when it needs your input.

**Note:** Chrome needs to be open for scheduled tasks to run. If your computer is off or Chrome is closed, Claude cannot do anything.

Good candidates for scheduling:

- Downloading weekly reports from dashboards
- Cleaning up your email inbox (archiving, starring, deleting spam)
- Checking for updates on sites you monitor
- Pulling metrics from tools that do not offer automatic exports

## Which Workflows Are Worth Automating?

Before you record everything, ask yourself three questions:

**1\. How often do you do this?** Daily or weekly tasks are worth automating. Something you do twice a year is not.

**2\. How many steps does it take?** Tasks with 3-15 consistent steps are ideal. Fewer than 3 and it is faster to just do it. More than 15 and it gets fragile.

**3\. Does it change every time?** If the steps are the same each time, automate it. If you make different decisions based on what you see, keep it manual or break it into smaller pieces.

**Good candidates:**

- Downloading reports from dashboards
- Filling out forms with the same information
- Navigating to specific pages
- Checking for updates
- Data entry tasks
- Routine admin work

**Bad candidates:**

- Tasks with lots of variation (different steps each time)
- Tasks that require judgment (deciding what to click based on what you see)
- Sites that change their layout often
- Sites that require solving CAPTCHAs
- Tasks involving sensitive credentials (Claude pauses at login screens anyway)

## Handling Logins

If your recorded task includes logging into a site:

- Claude records the navigation to the login page
- Claude records that you typed in the username and password fields
- Claude does NOT save your actual password
- When Claude replays, it will pause at the login and ask you to handle it manually
- If you are already logged in when Claude runs the shortcut (your session is still active), Claude skips the login and continues

**Tip:** Run your shortcuts while you are logged into the sites they use. This avoids the pause.

---

## Handling Things That Change

Some workflows break when a website changes. A button moves. A menu gets renamed. The page layout shifts.

Because you narrated what you were doing, Claude has a better chance of adapting. If you said "I'm clicking the download button" and the button moved to a different spot on the page, Claude can often still find it. Silent recordings break more easily because Claude only knows where you clicked, not what you meant.

If Claude still cannot find what it is looking for, it will stop and tell you. You have two options:

- Take over manually and finish the task
- Delete the old shortcut and record a new one

For sites that change constantly, recording might not be worth it. Use direct prompts instead.

---

## Combining Shortcuts with Prompts

You can run a shortcut and then ask Claude to do more:

```
Run "Download weekly sales report" and then summarize what the numbers show.
```

Or:

```
Run "Check new listings" and tell me if anything was posted in the last 24 hours.
```

The shortcut handles the navigation. Your follow-up prompt handles the thinking.

---

## Common Problems

**"Claude can't find the button"** The site layout might have changed. Re-record the shortcut. The button might be hidden behind a popup or banner. Close those manually first.

**"The recording missed a step"** Some actions happen too fast. Try doing the task more slowly when you record. Some sites use non-standard elements that Claude might not catch.

**"My shortcut stopped working"** The site probably changed. Record a new version. Your login session might have expired. Log in again before running.

**"Claude keeps asking me to log in"** Your session expired. Log into the site manually, then run the shortcut again. Some sites log you out aggressively. Not much you can do except stay logged in.

**"I scheduled a task but it didn't run"** Chrome was not open. Keep Chrome running for scheduled tasks. Your computer was asleep. Scheduled tasks need the machine awake.

## Tips for Better Recordings

- **Talk clearly.** You do not need to be formal. Just describe what you are doing as you do it. "I'm clicking this button to get to the reports page" is plenty.
- **Name what you see.** Instead of clicking silently, say "I'm clicking the Export button" or "I'm selecting the CSV option." This gives Claude landmarks it can find even if the layout shifts.
- **Do a clean run.** Before recording, do the task once to remind yourself of the steps. Then record it fresh.
- **Go at a comfortable pace.** Claude catches more when you are not rushing, and you explain better when you are not rushing either.
- **Use simple names.** "Monday report" is better than "Weekly sales report download from Acme dashboard v2."
- **Test immediately.** After saving a shortcut, run it right away to make sure it works.
- **Keep recordings short.** A 5-step shortcut is more reliable than a 25-step one. Break big tasks into smaller shortcuts if needed.

## Resources

**Workflow Documentation Template**

Use this to identify and document workflows before you record them. Fill it out, then use it as your script while recording.

```
WORKFLOW NAME:
[What do you call this task?]

TRIGGER:
[When do you do this? Daily? Every Monday? When X happens?]

STARTING POINT:
[What URL or app do you start from?]

STEPS:
1. [First thing you do]
2. [Second thing you do]
3. [Third thing you do]
...

END STATE:
[What does "done" look like? A downloaded file? A submitted form? Information on screen?]

VARIATIONS:
[Does anything change between runs? Different dates? Different inputs?]

WORTH AUTOMATING? (Check all that apply)
[ ] I do this weekly or more often
[ ] It takes 3-15 steps
[ ] The steps are the same each time
[ ] No CAPTCHAs or complex logins
[ ] The site layout is stable

If you checked 4-5 boxes: Record it.
If you checked 2-3 boxes: Maybe. Try it and see.
If you checked 0-1 boxes: Probably not worth it.
```
