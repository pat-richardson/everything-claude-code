## The Lesson

## Step 1: Open Gmail in Chrome

Go to [mail.google.com](http://mail.google.com/ "http://mail.google.com") and make sure you're logged in. You should see your inbox.

---

## Step 2: Open the Claude Sidebar

Click the Claude icon in your Chrome toolbar. The sidebar opens next to your inbox.

---

## Step 3: Ask Claude to Read Your Inbox

Start simple:

```
Summarize my unread emails.
```

Claude scans the visible emails and gives you a summary. Something like:

> "You have 12 unread emails:
> 
> - 3 from your team about the project deadline
> - 2 newsletters
> - 1 from your bank about a statement
> - 4 promotional emails
> - 2 from clients with questions"

This takes seconds. You now know what's waiting without clicking into each one.

You can go to another tab while Claude is doing this.

---

## Step 4: Get More Specific

Ask Claude to filter what it tells you:

```
Are there any emails that need a response today?
```

Or:

```
Show me emails from people I've emailed before, ignore newsletters.
```

Or:

```
What did Sarah send me this week?
```

Claude reads the inbox and answers based on what's there.

---

## Step 5: Have Claude Draft a Response

Pick an email you need to reply to. Open it so Claude can see the full thread. Then:

```
Draft a reply saying yes to the meeting and suggest Tuesday at 2pm.
```

Claude writes a draft. It doesn't send anything. You see the draft in the sidebar.

If you like it:

```
Put that in the reply field.
```

Claude pastes the text into Gmail's compose box. You review it, edit if needed, and hit send yourself.

---

## Step 6: Let Claude Handle Routine Replies

For emails that always get the same type of response:

```
Draft a polite decline for this sales pitch.
```
```
Write a short thank you reply for this introduction.
```
```
Say I'll get back to them next week with more details.
```

Claude writes the response. You approve and send.

## Working with Calendar

Open Google Calendar in Chrome ([calendar.google.com](http://calendar.google.com/ "http://calendar.google.com")). Open the Claude sidebar.

**See what's coming:**

```
What's on my calendar today?
```
```
What does my week look like?
```
```
Do I have anything scheduled Friday afternoon?
```

**Add events:**

```
Add a meeting called "Team sync" on Thursday at 10am for one hour.
```

Claude clicks through Calendar's interface and creates the event. Review it before you close the tab.

**Block time:**

```
Block 2-4pm every day this week as focus time.
```

Claude creates the events. You get protected time on your calendar.

**Move things:**

```
Move my Thursday meeting to Friday at the same time.
```

Claude finds the event, drags it (or edits it), and saves.

---

## Combining Inbox and Calendar

Claude can read an email and take action on your calendar:

```
Read the email from Mike about scheduling a call and add it to my calendar.
```

Claude reads the email, finds the proposed time, creates a calendar event, and shows you what it made.

Or:

```
Check my calendar and draft a reply to this email suggesting times I'm free next week.
```

Claude looks at your calendar, finds open slots, and writes a reply offering those times.

---

## Using "Ask Before Acting" Mode

For email and calendar, you probably want Claude to check with you before doing anything permanent.

In the Claude sidebar settings, enable "Ask before acting."

With this on, Claude will:

- Tell you what it plans to do
- Wait for you to approve
- Then do it

Example:

You: "Send a reply to this email saying I'm available Monday."

Claude: "I'll compose a reply saying you're available Monday and click Send. Proceed?"

You: "Yes" or "No, change it to Tuesday."

This keeps you in control. Nothing gets sent or scheduled without your approval.

## Setting Your Constraints

Email automation needs guardrails. Before you start, decide what Claude can and cannot do.

**What stays manual (you always handle these yourself):**

- Emails involving money or contracts
- Anything from your boss or key clients
- Messages that require judgment calls
- Personal or sensitive conversations
- Anything where the wrong reply would cause real damage

**What Claude can handle (with your approval):**

- Routine scheduling
- Simple confirmations
- Newsletter triage
- Drafting standard responses for your review

**What Claude can do automatically (if you trust it):**

- Archiving promotional emails
- Labeling by sender type
- Summarizing before you look

The key question: what happens if Claude gets this wrong? If the answer is "not much," automate it. If the answer is "real problems," keep it manual.

This is the same constraint-setting from the prompting framework (Lesson 1.3). The difference is that browser automation can click Send. Set your boundaries before you start.

## A Morning Inbox Routine

Here's how to use Claude for daily email triage:

1. Open Gmail and the Claude sidebar
2. "Summarize my unread emails and flag anything urgent."
3. Claude gives you the rundown
4. "Open the email from the client about the invoice."
5. Claude clicks into it
6. "Draft a reply saying I'll send the invoice by end of day."
7. Review the draft, send it
8. "Archive everything from newsletters."
9. Claude selects and archives them
10. Done in five minutes

---

## What Claude Can Do

- Read emails on screen
- Scroll through your inbox
- Open individual emails
- Draft replies (in the sidebar, not sent automatically)
- Paste text into compose fields
- Click Send (with your permission)
- Archive, delete, label, or move emails
- Read your calendar
- Create, edit, move, or delete events
- Check for conflicts
- Find free time

---

## What Claude Can't Do

- Access emails you haven't loaded (Claude sees what's on screen)
- Read attachments (Claude can see that attachments exist but can't open files)
- Send emails without your explicit approval (if "Ask before acting" is on)
- Access other people's calendars (unless they're shared with you and visible)
- Work when Chrome is closed

---

## Handling Sensitive Emails

For emails involving money, personal information, or work-critical decisions:

- Keep "Ask before acting" enabled
- Always review drafts before sending
- Don't ask Claude to handle anything you'd regret if it went wrong

Claude is helpful but not perfect. Read before you send.

## Scheduling Email Tasks

You can combine workflow recording (Lesson 2.3) with email:

1. Record yourself opening Gmail, archiving promotional emails, and flagging messages from a specific sender
2. Save it as "Morning inbox cleanup"
3. Schedule it to run at 8am every day

When you open your laptop, the busywork is already done.

## Common Problems

**"Claude can't see my emails"** Make sure Gmail is open and visible in the tab. Scroll if needed so emails appear on screen. Some emails collapse in threads. Click to expand them.

**"Claude drafted something wrong"** Edit it yourself before sending. Give Claude more context: "This person is a close colleague, keep it casual."

**"Claude clicked Send before I was ready"** Turn on "Ask before acting" mode in settings. Claude will always confirm before sending.

**"Claude missed some calendar conflicts"** Claude sees what's visible. If you have multiple calendars, make sure they're all displayed. Zoom out to see more of the week if events are hidden.

**"My email is not Gmail"** Claude works with other email sites too. The interface will be different, so Claude might be slower or less accurate. Try it and see.

---

## Tips for Better Email and Calendar Handling

- **Start with reading.** Get comfortable asking Claude to summarize before you ask it to take action.
- **Be specific about tone.** "Reply professionally" vs "Keep it casual" makes a difference in what Claude writes.
- **Use names.** "Email from Sarah about the budget" is clearer than "that email about money."
- **Review everything.** Claude drafts, you decide. Always read before sending.
- **Batch your requests.** Instead of handling one email at a time, ask Claude to triage first, then work through the queue.

## Resources

**Email & Calendar Automation Starter Config**

Copy these prompts and rules. Adapt them to your workflow.

**Morning triage prompt:**

```
Summarize my unread emails. Group them by:
1. Needs response today (from real people, time-sensitive)
2. Can wait (from real people, not urgent)
3. FYI only (newsletters, notifications, receipts)
4. Delete/archive (promotions, spam that got through)

Start with group 1.
```

**Calendar check prompt:**

```
What's on my calendar today? Flag any conflicts or back-to-back meetings with no break.
```

**Scheduling reply prompt:**

```
Check my calendar for next week. Draft a reply to this email offering 3 times I'm free for a 30-minute call. Keep the tone professional but friendly.
```

**End of day cleanup prompt:**

```
Archive all emails I've already replied to today. Archive all newsletters. Show me anything still unread that's from a real person.
```

**Constraint rules (save these somewhere Claude can see them):**

```
EMAIL RULES:
- Never send without my approval
- Never reply to anything involving money, contracts, or legal
- Never reply to [boss name] or [key client names] without showing me first
- Always ask before deleting (archive instead)
- Flag anything that looks urgent even if I didn't ask

CALENDAR RULES:
- Never delete events without asking
- Never schedule over existing events
- Always show me the event before creating it
- Protect 12-1pm as lunch (don't schedule over it)
```
